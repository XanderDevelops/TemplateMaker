What AI integration means inside CSVLink

This is not just a chatbot bolted onto an invoice tool. The AI is tied to the actual editor, so it can help generate the layout you need before you start linking live data.

Step 1

Describe the invoice

Tell AI what kind of invoice you want: service invoice, itemized invoice, agency invoice, consulting invoice, branded invoice, or a layout inspired by your own instructions.

Step 2

Generate the starting design

AI drafts the invoice structure with the right sections, headings, payment summary, line-item area, and general layout direction.

Step 3

Connect your table fields

Once the template exists, you link invoice number, dates, customer details, totals, and line-item fields from your spreadsheet into the layout.

Step 4

Review and export

Refine spacing, branding, and placement if needed, then export the invoices from your Excel or CSV data in batch.

Two strong ways to use AI in the invoice workflow

1. AI creates the template, you connect the data

This is the cleanest workflow for most teams. You use AI to build the invoice structure faster, then you drag your spreadsheet fields into the correct places and keep full control over the final mapping.

2. AI handles more of the setup for you

If you want to move faster, you can ask AI for a more complete setup draft so you spend less time designing from scratch and more time reviewing, adjusting, and confirming the result before export.

What kinds of instructions work well

Brand and tone instructionsExample: create a clean, modern invoice with my logo at the top left, a strong totals section, and a professional consulting feel.
Section instructionsExample: include bill-to details, issue date, due date, invoice number, an item table, subtotal, tax, total due, and payment terms.
Layout instructionsExample: keep the header compact, place totals on the bottom right, and leave room for multi-row line items.
Use-case instructionsExample: make it suitable for recurring monthly invoices, agency retainers, wholesale orders, or service businesses with itemized charges.
