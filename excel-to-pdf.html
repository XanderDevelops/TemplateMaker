When Excel to PDF actually makes sense

This is not just a file converter. A normal Excel to PDF export prints the spreadsheet itself. CSVLink is different: it uses Excel as the data source and your PDF as the design.

One layout, many rows

Perfect when every document should look the same, but each row has a different name, date, amount, address, or code.

Your PDF stays in control

Use the invoice template, certificate, label, form, letter, or report design your business already uses.

Built for printing and sending

Export a merged PDF for printing or individual PDFs in a ZIP when each customer or record needs its own file.

Common uses

Invoices

Place customer, invoice number, date, item, tax, and total fields onto your PDF invoice template.

Certificates

Fill names, programs, dates, award titles, and signer fields from a class or attendee spreadsheet.

Labels and forms

Use product data, addresses, IDs, notes, or internal fields to fill repeatable labels, slips, forms, and reports.
